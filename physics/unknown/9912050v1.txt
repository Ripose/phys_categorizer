Differential criterion of a bubble collapse in viscous liquids

Vladislav A. Bogoyavlenskiy*
Low Temperature Physics Department, Moscow State University, 119899 Moscow, Russia
(cid:126)Received 11 January 1999(cid:33)

The present work is devoted to a model of bubble collapse in a Newtonian viscous liquid caused by an initial
bubble wall motion. The obtained bubble dynamics described by an analytic solution signiﬁcantly depends on
the liquid and bubble parameters. The theory gives two types of bubble behavior: collapse and viscous
damping. This results in a general collapse condition proposed as the sufﬁcient differential criterion. The
suggested criterion is discussed and successfully applied to the analysis of the void and gas bubble collapse.

I. INTRODUCTION

Formation and collapse of bubbles in liquids are used in
many technical applications such as sonochemistry, litho-
tripsy, ultrasonic cleaning, bubble chambers, and laser sur-
gery (cid:64)1–4(cid:35). Bubble dynamics has been the subject of inten-
sive theoretical and experimental studies since Lord Ray-
leigh found the well-known analytic solution of this problem
for inviscid liquids (cid:64)5(cid:35). The advanced theory of cavitation
developed by Plesset gives the differential Rayleigh-Plesset
(cid:126)RP(cid:33) equation for the bubble radius R(t) (cid:64)6(cid:35). The RP equa-
tion describes the dynamics of a spherical void or gas bubble
in viscous liquids (cid:64)7–10(cid:35) and is also used as a ﬁrst approxi-
mation in more complex problems such as cavitation near
solid boundaries (cid:64)11–14(cid:35), collapse of asymmetric bubbles
(cid:64)15,16(cid:35), and sonoluminescence (cid:64)17–23(cid:35).

The main difﬁculties involved in theoretical investigations
of the RP equation are that (cid:126)i(cid:33) the solutions can be obtained
only numerically and (cid:126)ii(cid:33) the bubble wall velocity increases
to inﬁnity as the bubble collapses. Thus, computer simula-
tions of the bubble motion take a great deal of time and may
lead to signiﬁcant errors in the numerically calculated solu-
tions, especially when the bubble achieves supersonic
speeds. Unfortunately, the analytically described bubble dy-
namics was obtained only for the collapse in inviscid liquids
(cid:64)5(cid:35).

In this paper we present a way to avoid the above difﬁ-
culties for viscous liquids. The concept is based on the fact
that the RP equation is analytically integrable in the case of
the following restrictions: (cid:126)i(cid:33) the bubble is void and (cid:126)ii(cid:33) the
ambient hydrostatic pressure is absent. The imposed restric-
tions are valid as the bubble collapses because the gas and
the ambient pressures are negligible in comparison to the
velocity pressure at the bubble wall. The model gives an
analytic solution for the bubble radius R(t) and a collapse
criterion in the differential form. This differential criterion is
considered to be a sufﬁcient condition of the bubble collapse
in viscous liquids.

The present paper is organized as follows. In Sec. II the
general model of the void bubble collapse in a viscous liquid
caused by an initial bubble wall motion is formulated and
