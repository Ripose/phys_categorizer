Optimal set of 1046 rovibronic energy levels for 35 triplet electronic states of D2

has been obtained by means of statistical analysis of all available values of wavenum-

bers of rovibronic transitions studied in emission, absorption, laser and anticrossing

spectroscopic experiments of various authors. We used new method of the analysis

(Lavrov, Ryazanov, JETPLett., 2005), which does not need any a priory assump-

tions concerning molecular structure being based only on two fundamental princi-

ples: maximum likelihood and Rydberg-Ritz. The method provides the opportunity

to obtain the estimation of experimental errors of the wavenumbers independent

from those reported in original papers. From 3801 published wavenumber values 207

were excluded as blunders, wavenumber values of 122 from them were measured in

our experiments. The remaining set of 3716 experimental data has been divided into

14 subsets of uniformly precise data having close to normal error distribution. Un-

Introduction

2

It is well known that diatomic hydrogen being the simplest neutral molecule has most

sophisticated emission spectrum. Its band spectrum caused by spontaneous emission due

to electronic-vibro-rotational (rovibronic) transitions actually doesn’t represent visible, easy

recognizable band structure, but is looking like a multiline atomic spectrum. The pecu-

liarity of molecular hydrogen and its isotopic species - abnormally small mass of nuclei -

leads to high values of vibrational and rotational constants and large separation between

vibrational and rotational levels of various excited electronic states. As a result various rovi-

bronic spectral lines belonging to diﬀerent branches, bands and band systems are located in

the same areas of the spectrum. That leads to overlapping between various band systems,

bands and branches and mixing of rovibronic spectral lines having diﬀerent origin. On the

other hand, small mass of nuclei stimulates breakdown of Born-Oppenheimer approxima-

tion due to electronic-vibrational and electronic-rotational perturbations having regular and

irregular character. All this seriously complicate interpretation of the spectra of hydrogen

isotopomers and unambiguous identiﬁcation of rovibronic spectral lines. Therefore, most of

spectral lines in optical spectra of hydrogen isotopomers are not assigned up to now in spite

of tremendous eﬀorts of spectroscopists during last century [1, 2, 3, 4]. For example, in the

last compilation [4] of experimental data concerning molecular deuterium D2 the working

list of 27488 measured lines contains only 8243 assignments. The assignments were obtained

by traditional methods of analysis using wavenumber combination diﬀerences and Dunham

series expansions [1, 3], sometimes with comparison of molecular constants of diﬀerent iso-

topic species [2]. Later the results of ab initio and semiempirical calculations were taken

into account [4].

Nowadays, available information concerning energy level values of D2 molecule exists in

the form of the list of molecular constants for Dunham expansions in the NIST database [5],

and in the form of tables of rovibronic levels obtained in [4].

The Dunham coeﬃcients are known to provide very poor description of the rovibronic

energy level values. The data reported in [4] give in general rather good description of the

D2 spectrum, but they are also not free from criticism. The method of the analysis used in

[4] is based on common use of the combination diﬀerences, some selected wavenumbers for

certain transitions and multistage treating of separate band systems one by one. Therefore,

The method of the analysis does not need any a priory assumptions concerning molec-

ular structure being based only on two fundamental principles: maximum likelihood and

Rydberg-Ritz. The method diﬀers from known techniques in several aspects, namely, it: 1)

doesn’t need any assumptions concerning an internal structure of a molecule; 2) doesn’t in-

volve any intermediate parameters such as molecular constants in the traditional approach;

3) gives an opportunity to use in one-stage optimization procedure all available experimental

data obtained for various band systems, by various authors, and in various works; 4) pro-

vides the opportunity of rational selection of the experimental data in an interactive mode;

allow a user to eliminate rough errors, to revise wrong identiﬁcations of spectral lines and

to compare various sets of experimental data for mutual consistency; 5) allows independent

estimation of experimental errors and analysis of the shape of error distribution; 6) allows

to obtain not only an optimal set of rovibronic term values, but also the error bars caused

only by quantity and quality of existing experimental data [10].

The goal of the present paper is to report the results of applying the new method [8]

for statistical analysis of rovibronic spectral line wavenumbers of triplet band systems and

determination of optimal set of rovibronic energy levels for all known 35 triplet electronic

All available values of rovibronic transition wavenumbers studied in emission, absorption,

laser and anticrossing spectroscopic experiments of various authors [4, 6, 7, 11, 12, 13]

were analyzed. Fine and hyperﬁne structures of rotational levels were ignored.

In the

case of resolved and partly resolved ﬁne structure we used the wavenumber of most strong

component. The whole set of analyzed experimental data consists from 3716 spectral line

wavenumber values.

In the framework of this analysis the set has been divided into 14

subsets of uniformly precise data having close to the normal error distribution functions.

All the wavenumber data within each subset may be characterized by the same value of

standard deviation (SD). Estimated wavenumber error values of spectral lines are presented

in Table 1. Details of the analysis will be reported in our separate paper.

Experimental

The statistical analysis of the 3801 known to authors published wavenumber values has

shown that for the 207 spectral lines the wavenumber values have to be excluded as blun-

ders. 138 of those questionable lines are located within the wavelength range available for us.

Therefore we decided to provide independent experimental determination of their wavenum-

bers. For that purpose we used the emission spectra of D2 obtained during our studies of

translational and rotational temperatures in hydrogen and deuterium containing plasmas

[14]. Detailed description of the experimental setup was reported elsewhere [15]. Capillary

arc discharge lamps DDS-30 described in [16] have been used as a light source. They were

ﬁlled with about 6 Torr of the spectroscopically pure D2 + H2 (9:l) mixture. The range

of the discharge current was from 50 to 300 mA. The light from the axis of the plasma in

the capillary was directly focused by an achromatic lens on the entrance slit of the Czerny-

Turner type l m double monochromator (Jobin Yvon, U1000). The intensity distribution on

the focal plane of spectrometer was recorded by cooled CCD matrix detector of the Optical

Multichannel Analyser (Princeton Appl. Res., OMA-Vision-CCD System).

For identiﬁcation of D2 spectral lines in our spectrum we used the assignments and

wavelength values from [4]. Those values show certain spread around monotonic dependence

of the wavenumber from the distance along the direction of dispersion in the focal plane of

6

the spectrograph. For majority of strong lines this scatter was within error bars of 0.05 cm−1

reported in [4].

Then we supposed the dispersion of spectrograph to be monotonic function of the coordi-

nate in the focal plane of the spectrograph and got new wavenumber values of 122 spectral

lines slightly diﬀerent than those from paper [4]. 16 lines was too weak for detection in our

experiment. The new values often ﬁt better in determination of optimal set of rovibronic

energy level values. These new rovibronic spectral line wavenumber values are presented in

Table 3. Wavenumber values from paper [4] and observed minus calculated (O-C) values
for each wavenumber value (in cm−1) are also presented in the table. Calculated values are

diﬀerences of optimal energy values from Table 2. These O-C values show that our new data

are in better agreement with optimal set of energy levels. We estimated errors (standard
deviation SD) of our wavenumber values to be 0.06 cm−1 using the method of analysis of

experimental data (statistical estimation).

Finally we obtained 1046 rovibronic energy levels from 3716 values of experimental spec-

tral line wavenumber values.

Detailed comparison of the optimal set of rovibronic levels with the data reported in [4]

will be presented in our next paper. Two remarks should be made here. On one hand,
most of the diﬀerences between our data and those from [4] are less than 0.05 cm−1 reported

in [4] as an estimation of the one standard deviation uncertainty of the energy levels. On

the other hand, most of these diﬀerences are suﬃciently larger than SD error bars of our
