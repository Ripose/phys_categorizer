Eﬃcient Heating of Thin Cylindrical Targets
by Broad Electromagnetic Beams II

Andrey Akhmeteli∗

120
23525 Arlington Ave.
Torrance, CA 90501, USA
(Dated: November 17, 2006)

In Part I (physics/0405091), it was shown that it is possible to achieve eﬃcient heating of cylindri-
cal targets by electromagnetic beams with transverse dimensions that are several orders of magnitude
greater than those of the cylinder.

Part II contains derivation of the detailed conditions of eﬃcient heating in the longitudinal ge-
ometry and establishes a broader domain of parameters providing eﬃcient heating in the transverse
geometry.

One possible implementation using currently available technology is considered: a nanotube
(length 1 cm, diameter 15 nm) may be heated in the transverse geometry by a 30 femtosecond
pulse of a 50 GW Ti:Sapphire laser (wavelength 800 nm) to a temperature of 1 keV with heating
eﬃciency of 16 per cent. The resulting energy density exceeds 100 MJ/cm3.

An exact formula in the form of a one-dimensional integral is obtained for the amount of energy
absorbed in the cylinder heated by a gaussian electromagnetic beam in the longitudinal geometry.

V. EFFICIENT LASER HEATING OF
NANOTUBES

Let us discuss laser heating in the transverse geome-
try of carbon nanotubes (e.g. multi-walled nanotubes)
as the extreme case of ultrathin targets. The resulting
hot dense plasma may become a lasing medium for gen-
eration of coherent extreme ultraviolet (EUV) and soft
X-ray radiation. In our example, lasing may occur as a
result of recombination in H-like carbon rapidly cooled
by hydrodynamic expansion (Ref. [3]). Laser irradiation
of multiple nanotubes was previously used for X-ray gen-
eration (Ref. [4]). Let us show, however, that eﬃcient
laser heating of a single nanotube is also possible.

radiation is 800 nm (Ti:Sapphire laser) and the pulse

of the length of the nanotube.

VI. CONCLUSIONS

This Part II concludes the study (Ref. [1]) of condi-
tions of eﬃcient heating of a thin conducting cylinder
by a broad electromagnetic beam in the longitudinal and
transverse geometry.

As a spectacular application, eﬃcient heating of a nan-

otube by a femtosecond laser pulse is discussed.
